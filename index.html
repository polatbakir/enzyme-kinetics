ℹ️ BIO310 Enzyme Kinetics — Reference Guide

🧬 Beer-Lambert Law

A = ε · l · c

A = absorbance (unitless) · ε = molar absorptivity (43.6 M⁻¹cm⁻¹ for H₂O₂ at 240 nm) · l = path length (1 cm) · c = concentration (M)

Velocity conversion: v (mM/min) = v (ΔAbs/min) / (ε × l) = v (ΔAbs/min) / 0.0436

📐 Michaelis-Menten Equation

V = (Vmax · [S]) / (Km + [S])
  • V = reaction velocity (mM/min)
  • Vmax = maximum velocity at substrate saturation
  • Km = Michaelis constant — [S] at which V = Vmax/2
  • [S] = substrate concentration (mM)

📉 Lineweaver-Burk (Double-Reciprocal)

1/V = (Km/Vmax) · (1/[S]) + 1/Vmax

y-intercept = 1/Vmax → Vmax = 1/y-intercept

x-intercept = −1/Km → Km = −1/x-intercept

slope = Km/Vmax

📊 How to Read Km and Vmax

Km indicates enzyme-substrate affinity. Low Km = high affinity. High Km = low affinity, needs more substrate to reach ½Vmax.

Vmax reflects the catalytic capacity when all active sites are saturated.
